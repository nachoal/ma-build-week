# Five-person cold-viewer test

Status: protocol ready; external participants and results pending.

## Prompt

Show the frozen under-three-minute video once without prior explanation. Within
ten seconds of the closing frame ask:

> What did MA understand from the learner's recording, and what did it ask the
> learner to do next?

Then ask:

> Before the waiter spoke Japanese, did you understand the question and what
> the learner was supposed to answer?

Finally ask:

> Which actions were live learner/provider actions, and was any screen only a
> labeled replay or demonstration?

## Pass rule

At least 4/5 viewers independently communicate all of these ideas:

1. the objective and phrase meaning were clear before Japanese played;
2. MA reviewed the learner's actual voice approximately and gave a useful retry
   focus, without a score;
3. the waiter question and expected answer were understandable before the
   Japanese turn; and
4. any labeled historical replay was not live evidence.

A viewer who believes MA grades pronunciation/mastery, believes GPT-5.6 heard
the audio, or cannot identify the learner's next action reveals a comprehension
blocker even if other answers pass.
